Why It Matters

The Hidden Cost of an Unchecked Gas System

You probably don't think about your boiler or gas pipework until something goes wrong. But faulty gas appliances and ageing plumbing can cause serious harm — and serious bills — long before any visible damage appears.

Carbon Monoxide Poisoning

A poorly serviced or badly flued boiler can release odourless, deadly carbon monoxide into your home. An annual gas safety inspection catches the warning signs before anyone is harmed.

Invalid Home Insurance

Most home insurance policies require gas appliances to be serviced and certified. Without an up-to-date CP12 or boiler service record, your insurer may refuse to cover fire, flood or escape-of-gas claims.

Voided Boiler Warranty

Manufacturers like Worcester Bosch, Vaillant and Ideal require an annual service by a Gas Safe engineer to keep your warranty valid. Skip a year and a major boiler repair could fall on you.

Unexpected Repair Bills

Hidden leaks, hairline cracks in heat exchangers and corroded pipework can sit undetected for months. A routine inspection catches them early — before you are paying for a new boiler or flooded floor.

Landlord Compliance Fines

Under the Gas Safety (Installation and Use) Regulations 1998, landlords must hold a valid CP12 for every tenanted property. Non-compliance can lead to unlimited fines, prosecution and even imprisonment.

Property Sale Delays

Solicitors and buyers increasingly request a recent gas safety record before exchanging contracts. Without one, your sale could be delayed by weeks or fall through entirely.

Don't let an unchecked boiler catch you off guard.

Self-Check

Signs Your Boiler or Gas System Should Be Checked

Not sure whether your gas appliances need inspecting? If any of the following apply to your property, it is time to book a CP12 inspection.

  • !
    Your boiler is more than 12 months without a service

    Annual servicing is required to keep manufacturer warranties and insurance valid.

  • !
    Yellow or orange flame instead of crisp blue

    A lazy yellow flame can indicate incomplete combustion and a serious carbon monoxide risk.

  • !
    Black sooty marks on or around appliances

    Soot stains around a boiler, fire or hob mean exhaust gases are not venting safely.

  • !
    You are letting a property to tenants

    A valid CP12 Landlord Gas Safety Record is a legal requirement renewed every 12 months.

  • !
    Headaches, dizziness or nausea when heating is on

    Carbon monoxide symptoms ease when you leave the house. Get the gas system checked immediately.

  • !
    Damp patches, drips or low water pressure

    Visible leaks, wet pipework or sudden pressure drops point to plumbing faults that need fixing.
